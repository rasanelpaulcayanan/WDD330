Notes/Questions

When I was on Web Frontend 1, we always started the designs on a small viewpoint.
I didn’t understand the value of it that time other than being able to see my web creations on my phone.
Watching the video made me realize how important that mindset was.
It does make sense to create web pages based on the demand, and it looks like in the coming years people
will be using mobile devices more and more, while desktops get phased out.
In our company, they have started to migrate people from desktops to laptops.

I think password masking if vital for larger screen resolutions like laptops, desktops where the
likelihood of other people seeing your password is very high, especially if you’re in a public place.
if you’re using a mobile phone, it is easier for users to have an option to just hide it.
I can’t count the times I’ve been frustrated in having to login because I didn’t have an option to see the password I typed.

I didn’t know there was a way to just program an application or website to bring up the number pad automatically.
I think that’s a good suggestion I would like to include in my next website.

Am I the only user who just exits out of applications or websites because of excessive ads? Though I know that the inclusion of ads is necessary for revenue,
but annoying the users will not lead to additional conversion.

Question:
Question on Android and the newer IPhones Accessibility, if we put the most used icons/menus at the bottom of the screen wouldn’t that
risk the site or app from being closed? While it is true that it is easier to access the menu buttons when they
are at the bottom of the screen, for me I would still want them at the top.
Where a user wouldn’t accidentally hit the back or exit buttons.
